EX 01

Match the word to its definition

Choose the correct word for each definition.

  1. working for yourself, not for a company or a boss →
    ✔ self-employed
  2. doing jobs for different companies instead of having one employer →
    ✔ freelance
  3. working only some hours of the day or week →
    ✔ part-time
  4. lasting only for a limited time, not forever →
    ✔ temporary
  5. without a job →
    ✔ unemployed
  6. describing a job with no fixed end date →
    ✔ permanent
  7. working all the usual working hours of the week →
    ✔ full-time

EX 03

Rephrase — same meaning, new words

Rewrite each sentence using the word in CAPITALS. Keep the meaning the same.

  1. He works for himself. → SELF-EMPLOYED
    ✔ He is self-employed.
  2. She only works four hours a day. → PART-TIME
    ✔ She works part-time. / She has a part-time job.
  3. This job will only last three months. → TEMPORARY
    ✔ This is a temporary job. / This job is temporary.
  4. I manage the sales team. → IN CHARGE OF
    ✔ I am in charge of the sales team.
  5. It's her job to organize the meetings. → RESPONSIBLE
    ✔ She is responsible for organizing the meetings.
  6. He doesn't have a job at the moment. → UNEMPLOYED
    ✔ He is unemployed.
  7. I'm employed by a bank. → WORK FOR
    ✔ I work for a bank.
EX 04

Passive voice — job edition

Rewrite the sentences in the passive. You don't need to write "by them / by someone".

  1. They offered him a permanent job.
    ✔ He was offered a permanent job.
  2. The company employs 200 people.
    ✔ 200 people are employed by the company.
  3. Someone will interview the candidates tomorrow.
    ✔ The candidates will be interviewed tomorrow.
  4. They pay freelance workers every month.
    ✔ Freelance workers are paid every month.
  5. They have already chosen a new manager.
    ✔ A new manager has already been chosen.
  6. They are repairing my phone at the moment.
    ✔ My phone is being repaired (at the moment).
